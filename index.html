Project Overview

What is the Bone-on-a-Chip?

This project uses a microfluidic platform to control stem cell differentiation and biomineralization, advancing bone regeneration research through the integration of physical, chemical, and biological methods.

Technologies & Methods

  • Microfluidics for controlling solution environments
  • Computational modeling of calcium phosphate transformations
  • Real-time live-cell imaging for monitoring differentiation

Research Goals

Develop Biomimetic Materials

Design hydrogels and materials that support bone regeneration for non-healing defects.

Model Chemical Processes

Use computational models to simulate chemical transformations involved in bone formation.

Collaborate for Validation

Work with industry and research groups to validate biological processes and scale technologies.
